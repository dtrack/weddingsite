Getting to Tours

Our recommendation is to fly into Paris and take the train to Tours. Paris main airports are "Paris Charles de Gaulle" (CDG) and "Paris Orly" (ORY). CDG is more convenient because it has a TGV station (fast train) in the airport with direct trains to Tours. If you land in Orly you will have to take public transportation or a cab to the Paris Montparnasse train station in downtown Paris.

Moving around

Train is a great option and can take you anywhere in France. However, it can get pricy if you plan to visit a lot of places. Renting a car is probably a better option in this case. Please note that our standard rentals are stick shift , so be sure to request an automatic if you need. If you decide to rent a car it is about a 3 hour drive (as opposed to a little over an hour in train). Lastly, another option is to take the train to Tours and rent a car there.

Carpool may also be an option from Paris if we coordinate well with Jeremy's parisian friends. Please contact us if you would like to carpool from Paris.
On the wedding day there should also be plenty of carpool options for our international guests.

Booking a train

If you flew to CDG, put in CDG as your departure. If you flew to ORY, put in "Paris (PAR)" as your departure.
Put "Tours" as your destination.
For the results from Paris (not CDG) you will find 2 options to depart from: "Paris Austerlitz" and "Paris Montparnasse". Montparnasse only has TGVs that will take you to Tours in one hour. Austerlitz has slower (2-3 hours) but cheaper (11 to 40 euros) trains. In both cases you would have to travel from the Orly airport to the station downtown Paris.

Moving Around

Other than trains and rental cars, carpool is now mainstream in France and you can move around very cheaply. The leader is Blablacar. Otherwise you can still rent a car or take trains.

Resources:

  • Liligo: Car rental search engine
  • RentalCars: Car rental search engine, lets you filter out manual transmission (unfortunately automatic transmission car rentals are like double the price in France)
  • Blablacar: Carpooling site
  • Raileurope: train booking engine
